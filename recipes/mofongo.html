How to Make Mofongo

This a puerto rican delicacy that is made with an exotic fruit that is native to south america. This dish is perfect mix of sweet with salty richness.

Ingredients
    For Beef Broth
  • 2 tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 1 pound Beef Bones
  • 2 cloves Garlic(mashed)
  • 1 Red Onion (halved)
  • 4 cups Water
  • 1 sprig Cilantro
  • 1 sprig Cilantro Ancho
  • 1/4 teaspoon Oregano
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons Salt (or more to taste)
    • For Puerto Rican Mofongo
  • 1 cup Oil (for frying)
  • 5 unripe Plantains (peeled cut into "3/4 2 cm slices)"
  • 1 pound Pork Cracklings Pieces (cut into 1, 2.5cm)
  • 2 tablespoons Garlic (mashed)
Directions How to make Broth
  1. In a large saucepan heat the olive oil over medium heat. Brown the beef being careful that it does not burn.
  2. Add the garlic and onion and stir. Pour in 4 cups of water and add Cilantro Ancho, Cilantro, Oregano
  3. Boil for over an hour over low heat, topping off the water every once in while to maintain the same level.
  4. Season with Salt Sieve and remove the solids.
Puerto Rican Mofongo Steps
  1. Heat oil in small pot or saucepan over medium heat and the fry the plantain slices till golden brown all over (3-5). Remove from the oil and place on a paper towel
  2. Using a pilon or potato masher, crush the garlic and salt together, add in the plantains and mash.
  3. Add the pork cracklings and mash and crush to combine with the plantains.
  4. At this point the consistency will be more or less dense to form a ball.
  5. Shape the mixture into 6 balls and place each into a small plate.
  6. Place the balls into the serving plates, and garnish with the broth, that you'll use to pour over and moisten the mofongo balls. Enjoy!
